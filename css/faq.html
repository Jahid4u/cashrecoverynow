What are the biggest misconceptions about unclaimed funds?

Misconceptions and misunderstandings regarding unclaimed property matters are widespread. To dispel these notions, the most common misconceptions as well as their corrections are listed below:

Misconception Number 2: If people don't find their unclaimed property at their state's unclaimed property website, no money is owed to them so there's no need to look elsewhere.

Correction: Since there are thousands of funds nationwide that are not controlled by the states, people may indeed have money languishing in one or more of these unpublicized funds.

Misconception Number 3: Governments hold unclaimed money forever until it's claimed.

Correction: Each year government agencies escheat (confiscate) billions of dollars of unclaimed money.

Misconception Number 4: People can usually find their missing money on websites that charge for unclaimed property searches.

Correction: There is no nationwide or statewide database of all unclaimed funds.

Misconception Number 5: A phone call or letter about unclaimed funds is probably a scam.

Correction: When a reputable company informs people that their money is languishing in an unclaimed asset fund, they can be almost 100% certain that it's true.

What are unclaimed property funds?

These are escrow accounts controlled by government agencies that hold money until valid claims are filed by the rightful owners or a government deadline has passed, whichever comes first. The list of assets that are transferred to unclaimed property accounts is extensive. To name just a few, there are dormant bank accounts, insurance benefits, royalties, tax refunds, surplus auction proceeds, SEC refunds, government refunds, crime victim restitutions, inheritances and savings bonds that have stopped earning interest.
